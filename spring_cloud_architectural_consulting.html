One must know ins-and-outs of architectural design patterns of micro services software solutions. We are pioneers in architecting large, enterprise wide micro services based solutions with spring cloud. Over and above architecting the architectural components of micro-services, our expertise help our customers to select and map the right product / framework to the architectural components.

for e.g. whether the default implementation of Spring Cloud Config shall work best for centralized-configuration or Hashicorp Consul will be a better option.

Deliverables of Spring-Cloud Based Architecture

Vision Document

This document has 3 major sections viz. a) Problem Statement b) Stakeholder's Requests & c) The Application Features. This document illustrates the vision of the stakeholder's with respect to the enterprise solution that is being built.

Use Case Model

This provides the global view of actors and critical use cases that forms the part of the software solution. This helps to delimit the scope of the system.

Supplementary Specs

This document illustrates all the non-functional requirements of the system and also those functional requirements that are architecturally significant. Note that there are many requirements of any given software solution that has impact on the decisions you make on the architecture.

Architecture Document

This document lists all the architectural mechanisms and maps the same to the implementation mechanisms. We follow a typical 4+1 view of the software architecture model which entails - Logical View, Component View, Process View & Deployment View and center to all these views is the Use Case view that shows critical use cases.

Architecture Assessment and Review

Wants a 3rd eye to review the spring cloud based micro-services architecture that your team has already put in place ? We do review & assessment of spring cloud based micro-services architecture and present a report that help our customers to fine-tune.

We do the following & produce valuable deliverables when engaged for such an assignment.

We study the problem statement and meticulously identify requirements that are architecturally significant - the ones that has impact on the architecture. We check the level of detailing done by your team of such requirements to make sure that everything that may have impact on the architecture is well covered and detailed. In our experience, missing on details of architecturally significant requirements results in serious flaws and gaps in the architecture.

Usually the synergy between components / frameworks / APIs are not tested and proven. We do small PoCs keeping the focus on architectural requirements and highlight risks of using the product / framework in the architecture. For e.g. in one of the projects we reviewed, the architect had selected KIE Workbench as a solution to creating rules; we analyzed the rules related requirement and realized that the detailed level design of associating rules with the fields that needs to be processed will be overwhelming and tedious to maintain. An alternative (better) solution was suggested by us.

We check whether there are any better frameworks / products to replace the ones that are selected by your architects, do small PoC(s) and demonstrate the same.

We also take a deep-dive into the deployment architecture of your micro-services solution and provide our recommendations.

We finally produce a report that presents the recommendations on each and every aspect of the architecture - right from the logical view to the deployment view. In this report, we endorse what ever is already perfect and recommend where we see better approach.
